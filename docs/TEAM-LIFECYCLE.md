# Team lifecycle — how ideas become a real business

This is the **purpose framing** for work that started in the **Overnight Diary** (experiments + hourly iteration) and moves through **Possible Pipeline** into **Alive Management**.

## Three phases

### 1) Overnight Diary (experiments)
- **What:** Fast ideas, drafts, landing copy, Square tiers, distribution drafts under `~/clawd/overnight/`.
- **Goal:** Learn what is **sellable** and **ship-shaped**, not perfect.
- **Exit:** Good SKUs are **curated** into the public library (**`/pipeline/`** on this repo).

### 2) Possible Pipeline (internal project name — polish + constant outreach)
- **What:** One place on GitHub Pages (`/pipeline/`) lists **candidate products** under **public product names** with honest descriptions and **Square links by price tier** (not one link per SKU).
- **Goal:** Polish copy, proof, fulfillment SOP, and **distribute with steady external engagement** (posts, DMs, communities, later: paid acquisition when ready).
- **Exit:** A SKU is **repeatable**: you can sell it, deliver it, and measure it.

### 3) Alive Management (real customers)
- **What:** Active buyers, support, feedback loops, refunds handled, iteration from **customer truth**.
- **Goal:** Revenue + retention + referrals; SKUs that survive here are the **business**, not the side quest.

## Principles
- **Idea → dollars** beats idea → list.
- **Payments:** **Generic by price** — one Square Payment Link per tier; buyers choose the offer that matches that price; no requirement for SKU memos at checkout.
- **Fulfillment:** **Immediate** once Square confirms payment (email to the address Square collects), unless a specific SKU doc states otherwise and matches reality.
- **Naming:** **Possible Pipeline** is an **internal** phase name. **Public** copy uses normal product titles as published on the site.
- **Artifacts:** Overnight outputs stay in Clawd history; **canonical buyer-facing** list is **`/pipeline/`** on Pages.
